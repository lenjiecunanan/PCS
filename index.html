Group Chemistry

PCS’s group personalities are majorly composed of Extroverts, Intuitive, Feeling, and Judger.
Catherine has a personality of an Extrovert, Sensor, Thinker, and a Judger. She is natural when it comes to organizing, level-headed, articulate, and direct. While, Shashika and Jack almost have the same personality – Intuitive, Feeling, Judger, the only difference is Jack is an Introvert while Shashika is an Extrovert. Unlike Catherine, Jack and Shashika both lean on trusting their feelings rather than their mind but also follow their intuition rather than what their senses tell them.

The learning style of the group is the majority of Tactile and Visuals. Bank is however unique as he is both equally Tactile and Visual and is also slightly Auditory learner. The team mostly learns through touching and doing, they prefer "hands-on" learning where they can touch, move, build, or draw what they learned, and tends to learn better when some type of physical activity is involved. This means, as a team PCS must make learning fun and exciting. While working on projects, meetings and daily communication is important as they all learn from each other through visually explaining ideas. They will also require breaks often as they are active and tend to lose focus when stuck in one position at times.

According to the test, the working style of the group is greatly compatible. Catherine is a person who likes everything planned out, relationship-oriented, and realistic. She also does not admire people who waste her time. Shashika on the other hand, is also a strong-willed person, likes a good plan and communicates her ideas well and knows how to keep people engaged. They both inquire and collect information before they act. Jack is also a man of having core life goals which he works hard for. Jack likes to use his talents in a way that allows him to achieve success and feel positively about his contribution to the world. He is also great at developing unique ideas, the creative mind in the mostly detail-oriented group.
