IT TECHNOLOGIES

Internet of Things(IoT)

The Internet of Things is a system where the computer and digital devices interconnects through sensors and transmit data without human contact. This is an internetworking system where our daily life connects to exchange data through internet. This technology controls electrical equipment remotely and helps most of the industries in the world such as manufacturing, transportation etc.

Most people use IoT through their wearables such as fit bits and smartwatches where this technology is used as a wearable technology. IoT helps to communicate through devices where describes as machine to machine communication. IoT applications are another way to use this technology for the smart appliances at home where people can use to control their electrical equipment. Also, health care uses IoT applications to examine and analyse data for medical researches. Smart Cities are another concept which came through IoT. It helps people for their daily activities such as traffic controlling to ease the problems without happening accidents. IoT gave agriculture field a new concept named Smart Greenhouse where farmers can yield crops with less costs. Industrial Automation is another field where IoT applications come to work with logistics, inventory, factory digitalization, quality control and packaging.

In three years, IoT will help Automobile industry as they invested over $100 billion on self-driving cars development in last five years. Autonomous vehicles will be developed through IoT to transmit data through mechanical sensors and GPS with the technology to give the ultimate comfort to the driver. Cellular technologies such as NB IoT and Cat-M1 will be expanded through 5G network expansion according the reports from the Ericsson as the network expands the benefits will be on the reduction hardware prices and cellular technologies. As businesses are identifying the opportunities through IoT, the companies are investing more on this technology to gain profits in future. Industries such as clothing, healthcare, agriculture and municipalities are investing on IoT to have IT solutions through IoT networks. So, in future there will be more developed solutions for those industries other than in IT industry to help customers through their daily life. According to a research by McKinsey Global Institute, there will be a rapid growth in IoT as most companies are using this technology in modern ways such as tracking the supply chains using sensors. Home IoT market will grow making daily life easier with smart electrical appliances, entertainment devices, security systems etc. Moreover, manufacturing companies will start to use more IoT devices for their process to get best result from efficient energy and high level production with IoT machines which are capable of automated tasks.

IoT is consisted of hardware devices, software, communication and platforms. Hardware is the smart devices which IoT technology uses to gather data and communicate through. Software is the main component which works as the major part in the hardware devices which helps to gather data and transmit through IoT technology. Smart devices are connected through the communication to transmit collected data through connectivity solutions such as cellular, LAN, satellite or IoT environments such as ZigBee, Thread, Z-Wave, MQTT, LwM2M. These solutions do not work as only an option to send and receive data through cloud, where it works as a third party to communicate with other devices as well. IoT platforms are where that data collected, processed, analysed and transmitted through a user-friendly interference. Short range solutions like Bluetooth, RFID supports IoT technology to transmit data through devices such as wearables and helps to locate object positions in industries such as logistics through radio frequency identification. RFID has been developing to locate patients in healthcare industry to minimise the issue of out of stock situation in stores. Implementing these technologies can reduce costs and will help to get best from IoT technology for users.

PROJECT IDEAS

Fashion and dressing up is never easy for everyone. Some have good eyes on fashion and immediately know what to wear while others just grab the first thing they can get from their wardrobe, and the other bunch spends so much time thinking on what to wear. According to The Telegraph, a recent study found that the average woman, in the course of her lifetime, spends almost one year standing in front of her closet deciding what to wear. The study, which polled 2,491 women and was based on an adult lifetime from age 16 to 60, found that women spend an average of 16 minutes deciding what to wear on weekday mornings and 14 minutes on Saturdays and Sundays, for a whopping 287 days spread out over their lives.

  • Hitting the town on Friday night – 20 minutes
  • Christmas party or fancy dinner event – 36 minutes
  • Heading out of town for a vacation – 52 minutes

And on regular weekday mornings, most women try on at least two outfits before deciding what to wear to work.

This is why our group introduces *Noting to Wear*.
Noting to Wear as you guessed it is adapted from the common phrase we say when we have “nothing to wear”, which really just means having an exact outfit in my mind but does not own the exact pieces to put that outfit together. Noting to Wear is a fashion styling app that composes and suggests the users what to wear depending on the weather, their area of location while also considering their own collection of clothes.

NtW allows the user to create their own avatar, add their clothes collection through the QR code that is attached on the clothes when first bought or scan the clothes itself to allow the app to collect the form and color of the users’ dress or shirt. The application also encourages the user to allow the app to collect their location, in order for the app to figure the weather of that area and its fashion trends. The user is allowed to choose and input the occasion or the purpose of their dressing up.

For example, the user has a date with her partner on Thursday night. She inputs the location of where she and her partner will have their date, it then allows the app to collect the weather of the area. Since she is a new user, she scanned her clothes to build her clothes collection in the app, she also scanned the QR codes of the dresses she just bought. So now, the app has all the information it requires to suggest the user what to wear.

  • Occasion: Date night
  • Location: Melbourne CBD
  • Weather: 32C Warm
  • When: Thursday 25th
The app will then suggest something like this.

The user’s avatar is now wearing an outfit appropriate to the suggested inputs.
